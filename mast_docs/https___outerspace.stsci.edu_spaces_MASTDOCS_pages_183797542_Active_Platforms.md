#  [Active Platforms](https://outerspace.stsci.edu/spaces/MASTDOCS/pages/183797542/Active+Platforms)
  * Created by  ![User icon: tdutkiewicz](https://outerspace.stsci.edu/s/aneund/9204/1jtwilc/_/images/icons/profilepics/anonymous.svg) [Thomas Dutkiewicz](https://outerspace.stsci.edu/display/~tdutkiewicz%0A) on [Apr 11, 2023](https://outerspace.stsci.edu/pages/viewpreviousversions.action?pageId=183797542 "Show changes") 1 minute read 


Currently, STScI has two active JupyterHub platforms: [JWebbinar](https://jwebbinar.science.stsci.edu), which supports JWST training, and [TIKE](https://timeseries.science.stsci.edu), which enables time series analysis and machine learning related to NASA's TESS and Kepler missions. While both hubs rely on similar infrastructure to host multiple JupyterLab sessions, the pre-installed software and general usage are quite different. As a training site, [JWebbinar](https://jwebbinar.science.stsci.edu) has hosted numerous classes teaching students how to use various key applications for JWST, including the [jwst calibration software](https://github.com/spacetelescope/jwst) and [Jdaviz visualization tools](https://github.com/spacetelescope/jdaviz). In contrast, [TIKE](https://timeseries.science.stsci.edu) is used for active research due to its convenient software, documentation, and extensive time series data.

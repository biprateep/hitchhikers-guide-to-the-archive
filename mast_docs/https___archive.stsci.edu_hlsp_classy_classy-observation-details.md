##  GFWHM Information
##### GFWHM (Gaussian Full Width Half Maximum)
When possible, the primary science aperture (PSA)+MIRRORA was used for the COS NUV acquisition images. For bright targets (> 2x10-15 erg/cm2/s/A), MIRRORB was used to attenuate the flux and protect the detector. This results in significantly lower counts in the CLASSY acquisition images and, subsequently, poor spatial light profile fits. For this reason, no GWFHM values are reported in some cases when MIRRORB is used.
##  Lifetime Position Information
##### Lifetime Position
The COS Far Ultraviolet (FUV) detector has periodically moved lifetime positions to mitigate the effects of gain sag and extend the longevity of the detector ([COS Instrument Handbook; Sec 2.1](https://hst-docs.stsci.edu/cosihb/chapter-2-proposal-and-program-considerations/2-2-essential-questions-for-preparing-cos-observations)). Currently, the FUV detector is in the fourth lifetime position (LP4), but will be moving to the fifth lifetime position (LP5) in October 2021. For the most updated information on lifetime position, see [the COS2025 policies page](https://www.stsci.edu/hst/instrumentation/cos/proposing/cos2025-policies).
The COS Near Ultraviolet (NUV) detector does not suffer from gain sag, and therefore has not moved position over time. Observations using the NUV detector (e.g., G185M observations) will have a recorded Lifetime Position of N/A.
##  Acquisition Image Information
##### Acquisition Image
When possible, the primary science aperture (PSA)+MIRRORA was used for the COS NUV acquisition images. For bright targets (> 2x10-15 erg/cm2/s/A), MIRRORB was used to attenuate the flux and protect the detector. It should be noted that MIRRORB produces a secondary image that is half the intensity of the primary image and is displaced by 20 pixels (about 0.5 arcsec) in the along-dispersion direction.

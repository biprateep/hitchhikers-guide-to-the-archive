Please note that this feature is available exclusively in the HST and JWST search forms. We are working on deploying this feature to other missions, so stay tuned! 
# Accessing Image Preview Results
![Underneath an "Edit Search" button, two tabs read "Results" and "Previews".](https://outerspace.stsci.edu/download/thumbnails/352354485/previews_selected.png?version=1&modificationDate=1759174759652&api=v2)
By default, after a search you are taken to the [tabular search results page](https://outerspace.stsci.edu/spaces/MASTDOCS/pages/107840287/Search+Results+Page). You can change this selection to 'Preview' to instead see image previews for your matching observations.
* * *
# Result Cards
Each result "card" consists of a few elements, outlined below:
![The image "tile", showing the metadata, preview controls, and the preview image itself.](https://outerspace.stsci.edu/download/attachments/352354485/new-image-result.png?version=1&modificationDate=1759521534181&api=v2)
## (1) Selection Checkbox
When clicked, the selection checkbox selects a dataset. You can then [download corresponding files or export metadata to a table](https://outerspace.stsci.edu/spaces/MASTDOCS/pages/107840287/Search+Results+Page#SearchResultsPage-download_button).
Selection Synchronization
Your selections are synchronized between the tabular results and the preview results. Your choices will be preserved when switching between the two!
## (2) Dataset Metadata
The metadata section shows information shared across **the entire dataset**. This consists of a unique dataset ID, the instrument used, and the coordinates of the observation. Product Level and Optical Element may contain multiple values, separated by commas.
If the dataset is marked as exclusive access, it will be shaded yellow in both the tabular results and the preview results.
## (3) Mini-Preview Controls
The controls for the mini-preview display the number of images available and which one is currently being shown (e.g. "`3/10 previews`").
![Expand symbol.](https://outerspace.stsci.edu/download/thumbnails/352354485/expand-preview.png?version=1&modificationDate=1755116791510&api=v2) The "expand preview" button opens the full-size version of the image currently shown as a mini-preview, in a new tab.
![Left arrow symbol.](https://outerspace.stsci.edu/download/thumbnails/352354485/left-arrow.png?version=1&modificationDate=1755117339362&api=v2)![Right arrow symbol.](https://outerspace.stsci.edu/download/thumbnails/352354485/right-arrow.png?version=1&modificationDate=1755117342134&api=v2) Previous/next controls on the mini-preview image. Pressing the left arrow decreases the index of the current image (`3/11` → `2/11`) while pressing the right arrow increases it (`4/6 → 5/6`).
**Mini-Preview Possible Results**
![Image preview showing a spiral galaxy.](https://outerspace.stsci.edu/download/thumbnails/352354485/valid-photo.png?version=1&modificationDate=1755118366034&api=v2)
![Yellow-shaded image preview reading: "No Preview Available", "Exclusive Access Data", and "If you have access, Login In to preview and download."](https://outerspace.stsci.edu/download/thumbnails/352354485/exclusive_access_yellow.png?version=1&modificationDate=1759173578280&api=v2)
![Image preview showing a "null result" for which there are no previews.](https://outerspace.stsci.edu/download/thumbnails/352354485/null-result.png?version=1&modificationDate=1755118365873&api=v2)
## (4) Mini-Preview and Metadata
The mini-preview shows a small version of the preview image, along with its filename and calibration level. There are three possible results:
  1. **(Top)** The preview image is displayed, along with its metadata. You can use the controls in (1) or (3) for to manipulate the preview.
  2. **(Center)** The metadata are displayed, but the preview is exclusive access data. If you are authorized by the Principal Investigator of the program, you can click "log in" to authenticate and see the preview.
  3. **(Bottom)** No images or metadata are displayed. This dataset has no files that can be displayed as a preview.


You can also click anywhere on the mini-preview to open a multi-preview display.
* * *
![A multi-preview display, showing the first 12 images out of 400, arranged in a grid with a section for dataset-wide metadata at the top. Each image is marked with a Filename and a Level..](https://outerspace.stsci.edu/download/attachments/352354485/big-preview.png?version=1&modificationDate=1755423800480&api=v2)
# Multi-Preview Display
After clicking on a mini-preview, a multi-preview display will open. This enables you to see multiple previews for the same dataset simultaneously.
Since there is more room available to display metadata, you may also find this useful for observations that have many optical elements. In the standard view, long fields are truncated.
